<p><p>Novel oral anticoagulants drugs are classified into two main types: Direct Thrombin Inhibitors and Factor Xa Inhibitors. Direct Thrombin Inhibitors work by directly inhibiting the activity of thrombin, a key enzyme involved in the blood clotting process. On the other hand, Factor Xa Inhibitors target factor Xa, another key enzyme in the blood clotting cascade. Both types of drugs are used to prevent and treat blood clots, stroke, and other conditions related to abnormal blood clotting.</p></p>
