<p><ul><li>Active IR Focus</li><li>CCD AF</li><li>Phase Difference Measurement Focus</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Low light cameras are of different types such as Active IR Focus, CCD AF, and Phase Difference Measurement Focus. Active IR Focus uses infrared light to improve focus in low light conditions. CCD AF adjusts the lens position to achieve clear focus in low light. Phase Difference Measurement Focus uses multiple sensors to detect contrast differences and quickly achieve focus. These different technologies cater to varying needs and preferences of users when capturing images or videos in low light situations.</p></p>
