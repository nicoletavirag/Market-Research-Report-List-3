<p><ul><li>External Fixation Device</li><li>Internal Fixation Device</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Medical orthopedic fixation devices are used to stabilize and support bone fractures or joint injuries. External fixation devices are attached externally to the body and hold the bones in place using pins and rods. Internal fixation devices are placed inside the body during surgery and include plates, screws, and rods to stabilize the fractured bone. Both types of devices are crucial in orthopedic surgeries to promote proper healing and recovery for patients with bone injuries.</p></p>
