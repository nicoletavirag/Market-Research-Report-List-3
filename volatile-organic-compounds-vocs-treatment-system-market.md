<p><p>Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) treatment systems include two main types of technologies: destructive treatments and non-destructive treatments. </p><p>Destructive treatments involve breaking down VOCs into simpler molecules through processes like incineration or oxidation. These methods are effective but can be costly and produce secondary pollutants.</p><p>Non-destructive treatments, on the other hand, focus on capturing and removing VOCs without breaking them down. Examples include adsorption, absorption, and membrane filtration. These methods are often more cost-effective and environmentally friendly compared to destructive treatments.</p></p>
