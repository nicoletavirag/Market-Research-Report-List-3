<p><p>Phase Shifting Transformers (PST) are used to control the flow of power in electrical grids by adjusting the phase angle between input and output voltages. There are two main types of PSTs based on their core structure: Direct (One Core) PST and Indirect (Two Cores) PST. Direct PSTs have a single core for both primary and secondary windings, while Indirect PSTs have separate cores for each winding. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring efficient power transmission and distribution in electrical networks.</p></p>
