<p><p>Surface active agents, also known as surfactants, are classified into four main types based on their ionic nature: anionic, cationic, zwitterionic, and non-ionic surfactants. </p><p>Anionic surfactants are negatively charged and commonly used in household cleaners and personal care products. Cationic surfactants have a positive charge and are often found in hair conditioners and fabric softeners. Zwitterionic surfactants have both positive and negative charges, making them versatile and suitable for a wide range of applications. Non-ionic surfactants do not carry any charge and are gentle, making them ideal for sensitive skin products.</p></p>
