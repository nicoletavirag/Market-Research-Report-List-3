<p><p>Residential single-phase electricity smart meters are classified into two main types: IC card and non-IC card markets. IC card smart meters require users to insert a card to prepay for electricity usage, allowing for better control and budgeting. Non-IC card smart meters do not require a card and typically involve post-payment methods. Both types offer benefits such as real-time monitoring, remote reading, and improved efficiency. The choice between IC card and non-IC card meters depends on the specific needs and preferences of the consumer.</p></p>
